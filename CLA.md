# Contributing & the Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

This project is **dual-licensed**: AGPL-3.0-or-later for everyone, plus a commercial license
for organisations that need to keep their own work closed (see [`COMMERCIAL.md`](COMMERCIAL.md)).

Dual licensing only works if **a single party holds the rights to relicense the whole
codebase**. So that the project can keep offering the commercial license, every contribution
must come with a grant that lets the maintainer license your contribution under *both* the
AGPL **and** the commercial terms.

## The agreement

By submitting a contribution (a pull request, patch, or any code/content) to this project, you:

1. **Certify** you wrote it, or otherwise have the right to submit it, and that you're entitled
   to grant the rights below (Developer Certificate of Origin, https://developercertificate.org).
2. **Grant** the project maintainer a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free,
   irrevocable license to use, reproduce, modify, and distribute your contribution, **and to
   sublicense it under any license — including the AGPL-3.0-or-later and proprietary/commercial
   licenses** offered by the project.

You retain copyright in your contribution; this is a license grant, not an assignment.

## How to sign

Add a `Signed-off-by: Your Name <you@example.com>` line to each commit (`git commit -s`). That
sign-off, together with this document, constitutes your agreement to the above for that
contribution. For substantial or corporate contributions we may ask for an explicit signed CLA
— open an issue and we'll arrange it.

## Without a sign-off

Contributions without a sign-off can't be merged, because they'd break the project's ability to
offer the commercial license. Thanks for understanding.
